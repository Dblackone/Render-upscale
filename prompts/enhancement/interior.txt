INTERIOR ARCHITECTURAL ENHANCEMENT:

LIGHTING HIERARCHY:
- Establish natural daylight as primary light source — window light creates soft, directional illumination
- Layer artificial lighting: ambient, task, and accent lighting working together
- Realistic light spill and bounce — light from windows should illuminate adjacent surfaces
- Shadows cast by furniture and objects must be soft, accurate, and grounded
- Ceiling lighting should create natural hot spots and gradient falloff
- Avoid flat, even illumination — create lighting depth and atmosphere

MATERIALS AND SURFACES:
- Floor materials (timber, stone, carpet, concrete) must show realistic surface texture and reflectivity
- Walls show paint texture, plaster variation, subtle sheen
- Joinery and furniture materials — timber grain, fabric weave, leather texture, metal finishes
- All surfaces show appropriate scale of detail

FURNITURE AND OBJECTS:
- Furniture appears solidly constructed with correct weight and material
- Soft furnishings (cushions, rugs, curtains) show natural draping, folds, and gravity
- Decorative objects, books, plants — add lived-in character
- Styling objects add personality and scale reference

ATMOSPHERE AND MOOD:
- Photographic depth of field — nearest objects slightly soft, main space sharp, background fades
- Warm, inviting color temperature for residential; clean, crisp for commercial
- Window views show realistic exterior environment

HUMAN PRESENCE (if requested):
- 1-3 people naturally occupying the space
- Activities that match the space type (reading, dining, working)
- Correctly scaled to furniture and space
