LANDSCAPE AND GARDEN ENHANCEMENT:

VEGETATION — HIGHEST PRIORITY:
- Replace all proxy/placeholder vegetation with photorealistic plant species
- Species selection must match climate zone and design intent
- Trees: species-specific form, bark texture, leaf color variation, natural asymmetry, correct seasonal state
- Shrubs: natural mounding form, leaf texture, seasonal flowering where appropriate
- Ground cover: grass texture with natural variation, moss, mulch, planted ground layers
- Climbers and wall plants where contextually appropriate
- Plants must look healthy, thriving, and appropriately scaled to their context

WATER FEATURES:
- If present: realistic water surface with correct reflections, ripples, and movement suggestion
- Water edges show realistic interface with paving or planted edges

HARD LANDSCAPE:
- Paving materials show natural jointing, weathering, lichen or moss growth in joints
- Gravel paths have natural texture and depth
- Timber decking, walls, and structures show appropriate weathering and material quality

LIGHTING IN LANDSCAPE:
- Golden hour or soft overcast lighting enhances planting most naturally
- Dappled light through tree canopy on ground surface
- Directional sunlight creates depth through shadow casting

HUMAN PRESENCE:
- People occupy the landscape naturally: sitting, walking, relaxing
- Children playing where contextually appropriate
- Scale figures confirm the human dimension of the space

SKY AND ATMOSPHERE:
- Beautiful, photographic sky appropriate to season and region
- Soft atmospheric haze in distant tree canopy
