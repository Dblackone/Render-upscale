BIM AND TECHNICAL RENDER ENHANCEMENT:

MATERIAL APPLICATION:
- Apply photorealistic materials to all building elements based on their likely construction
- Structural elements: concrete with formwork texture, steel with appropriate finish
- Envelope: glazing, cladding, roofing applied with realistic material properties
- Interior elements: flooring, ceiling, wall finishes appropriate to space type

SPATIAL CLARITY:
- Enhance spatial hierarchy — primary spaces more prominent than secondary
- Lighting differentiates inside from outside, public from private
- Material contrast helps read spatial organization clearly

CONTEXT AND SETTING:
- Add a realistic site context: sky, ground plane, basic landscape
- Neighboring buildings or landscape as a simple, non-distracting background
- Ground plane shows realistic surface material

LIGHTING FOR LEGIBILITY:
- Primary lighting direction helps read massing and three-dimensional form
- Interior illumination visible through glazing
- Shadows clarify form and depth

HUMAN SCALE:
- Add 2-5 people at key locations to establish scale
- People are positioned to convey how spaces are intended to be used

NOTE: Preserve all structural, spatial, and geometric information completely. This is a design communication tool. The goal is to make the design legible and compelling without redesigning anything. Improve presentation quality only.
